1. Scope and controller

This Policy explains how the PlanIt web application and PlanIt-hosted pages use cookies and related technologies that store information on, or access information from, a browser or device. Related technologies include local storage, session storage, IndexedDB, browser cache, SDK identifiers, and temporary authentication state.

The Service is operated by the independent developer publishing under the name Artomk, based in Ukraine (“PlanIt,” “we,” “us,” or “Controller”). Contact: planit.app.support@gmail.com.

This Policy supplements the Privacy Policy. The Privacy Policy explains the personal data processed through PlanIt, purposes and legal bases, recipients, international transfers, retention, security, and your rights. Server-side databases, hosting logs, and files stored in Firebase are not browser cookies, although related requests may involve identifiers described here.

2. What cookies and similar technologies are

  • Cookies are small text values associated with a website and stored by a browser. First-party cookies belong to the website you visit; third-party cookies belong to another domain.
  • Local storage stores key/value data for an origin and normally remains after the browser is closed until the website or user removes it.
  • Session storage is normally limited to the current browser tab or session.
  • IndexedDB is a browser database used for larger or structured records such as cached files and certain Firebase SDK state.
  • Browser cache can retain static application files to improve performance. Cache behavior and expiry may be controlled by the browser and hosting response headers.
  • SDK or installation identifiers are values generated by a software provider to distinguish an app installation or browser instance. They can perform a cookie-like function even when they are not stored in a cookie.

Privacy rules can apply to all of these technologies, not only files literally named “cookies.”

3. How consent works on the web

When the PlanIt web app has no current choice for your browser, optional Firebase Analytics remains off and a notice offers Reject analytics and Accept analytics at the same level. Merely scrolling, closing a browser tab, or continuing to use PlanIt is not treated as analytics consent. There is no preselected analytics option.

If you accept, PlanIt initializes Firebase Analytics and permits analytics storage. Advertising storage, advertising user data, and ad personalization remain denied in the web Analytics consent configuration. If you reject, PlanIt does not initialize Firebase Analytics or intentionally send web Analytics events. Strictly necessary storage continues because it supports functionality or security you request.

Where EU ePrivacy rules or the UK Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations apply, PlanIt relies on consent before using or accessing optional Analytics technologies. Where Analytics also processes personal data under the GDPR or UK GDPR, the corresponding legal basis is consent. Strictly necessary storage does not rely on cookie consent; related personal-data processing may instead be necessary to provide the requested Service, protect the Service and users, or meet legal obligations, as described in the Privacy Policy. Accepting PlanIt's Terms, creating an account, or choosing Guest Mode is not Analytics consent.

PlanIt stores the choice in planit_cookie_consent_v1 for up to 12 months. The record contains only the policy version, whether analytics was accepted or rejected, the time of the choice, and its expiry; it does not contain an account ID, email address, random consent ID, or advertising identifier. If browser storage is unavailable, the choice may apply only to the current page and may need to be made again.

You can withdraw or grant analytics consent at any time through the persistent Cookie settings control in the web app or through About App → Cookie Settings. Withdrawal disables future PlanIt Analytics collection and attempts to remove first-party Google Analytics cookies accessible to the current origin. It does not retroactively erase lawfully collected records already held by Google; see the Privacy Policy for deletion and objection rights.

5. Optional Firebase Analytics

After analytics consent, PlanIt initializes Firebase Analytics for the web and records screen-view events. Google Analytics may also automatically process session, engagement, browser/device, timestamp, referring page, approximate location derived from IP address, and technical identifiers. PlanIt's current custom web event call sends the route/screen name; it is not designed to send schedule text, attachment contents, passwords, or authentication tokens.

6. Provider-controlled technologies and third-party pages

When you actively open Google or Apple sign-in, the provider's domain may read or set its own cookies or similar technologies to authenticate you, prevent fraud, remember provider-level settings, and secure the session. PlanIt cannot list or control every cookie already present in your Google or Apple account session, and those technologies follow the provider's policy:

  • How Google uses cookies;
  • Google Privacy Policy;
  • Apple Privacy Policy;
  • Privacy and Security in Firebase; and
  • Google Analytics cookie usage.

A user-created schedule link can open an unrelated third-party website. That destination may use its own cookies and is not controlled by PlanIt. Check the destination before continuing.

7. How to manage or delete browser storage

Change PlanIt's analytics choice

  1. Open the PlanIt web app.
  2. Select the persistent Cookie settings control, or open Settings → About App → Cookie Settings.
  3. Select Accept analytics or Reject analytics. The new choice applies to future collection from that browser profile.

Use browser controls

Most browsers allow you to view, block, or delete cookies and site data for a particular origin. Clearing PlanIt site data can remove the consent record, Firebase sign-in persistence, local or Guest Mode schedules, preferences, and cached attachments. Export anything you need and sign out before clearing data on a shared device.

Private browsing, tracking protection, storage partitioning, extensions, or disabled cookies/IndexedDB may reduce Analytics but can also interfere with Google/Apple sign-in, persistent sessions, attachment previews, or local schedules. Browser controls operate separately for each browser profile and device.

Deleting cookies is not account deletion.

Clearing browser storage does not remove a PlanIt cloud account or cloud data. Use the Account & Data Deletion page for account deletion.

8. Native Android and iOS apps

Native PlanIt apps do not use browser cookies in the same way as the web app. They use app storage, secure authentication state, Firebase app-instance or installation identifiers, local notification data, cached attachments, and—where applicable—mobile advertising identifiers or provider SDK storage. Native builds currently use Firebase Analytics and request non-personalized AdMob banners outside Expo Go. The Privacy Policy describes that processing and the current limitations of native advertising/analytics choices.

The web cookie preference controls only web Firebase Analytics for the browser profile where the choice is made. It does not automatically change native app settings or Google/Apple account-level preferences.
