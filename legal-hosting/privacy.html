Privacy at a glance
  • Guest schedules are kept on the device and are not uploaded to PlanIt's account database. Native advertising/analytics, crash reporting, update, and basic network services may still process technical data. Optional web Firebase Analytics remains off until the browser user accepts it.
  • When you use an account, PlanIt uses Firebase to authenticate you and synchronize your schedules, settings, devices, notifications, and cloud attachments.
  • Native builds request non-personalized ads. Google may still use device or advertising identifiers for purposes such as frequency capping, fraud prevention, and aggregated reporting.
  • PlanIt does not request GPS/location permission and does not currently use AI to analyze your content. IP addresses and file metadata may nevertheless reveal an approximate or embedded location.
  • Account deletion is available, but an in-app deletion attempt cannot by itself prove that every provider-side object has been erased, and it cannot remotely erase local copies, recipients' copies, or data that a provider must retain temporarily. See the detailed Account Deletion page.

1. Scope and controller

This Privacy Policy explains how the PlanIt mobile and web applications, the PlanIt account and synchronization services, and the PlanIt-hosted legal pages (together, the “Service”) handle personal data.

The Service is operated by the independent developer publishing under the name Artomk, based in Ukraine (“PlanIt,” “we,” “us,” or “Controller”). Questions and privacy requests may be sent to planit.app.support@gmail.com.

This Policy does not replace the privacy policies of Google, Apple, Expo, your operating-system provider, your app store, or a third-party website that you open from a user-created link. Those parties may process data as independent controllers under their own terms.

2. Data we process

The data processed depends on the platform, the features you use, the permissions you grant, and whether you use Guest Mode or a registered account.

Account and authentication data
Email address, display name, Firebase user ID, email-verification status, sign-in provider and provider identifiers, authentication/session tokens, and account-security events. If you use email/password sign-in, Firebase Authentication handles the password; PlanIt does not have normal access to your plaintext password. Google or Apple may provide a name and email address that you authorize, including an Apple private-relay address.
Schedules, tasks, and settings
Schedule names and structure; subjects; dates and times; rooms and buildings; teacher names, email addresses, and phone numbers; links; notes; tasks and due dates; reminders; themes, language, navigation, and other preferences; synchronization versions, timestamps, and deletion markers.
Files and attachments
Photos and documents you select, their contents, file names, MIME types, sizes, storage paths, tokenized download URLs, upload timestamps, preview information, compression information, and local cache records. Some processed images are re-encoded and marked as having metadata removed, but this is not guaranteed for every image or document. Originals may contain EXIF, author, document-history, or location metadata.
Device, session, and security data
A pseudonymous device key derived from your user ID and device/browser characteristics; device name, brand, model, platform and operating system; browser user-agent on web; public IP address; last login, last seen, and last synchronization times; push-permission status; Expo push token; and device-removal state. The pseudonymous device key is not anonymous because it is stored under your account.
Notifications
Notification preferences, locally scheduled reminder identifiers, login-notification content and status, device name, platform, IP address, timestamps, read status, and push-delivery tokens. Lesson reminders are generally scheduled locally; account-login alerts may be stored in Firestore and delivered through Expo Push Services.
Shared-schedule data
Share code, owner ID, optional owner name, schedule name, selected schedule fields, creation time, chosen expiry time, and active status. Depending on your sharing selections, a shared schedule may include teachers' contact details, links, notes, rooms, buildings, and reminder settings. PlanIt sanitizes the shared copy and does not intentionally include tasks or cloud attachments in that copy.
Usage, advertising, and diagnostic data
App instance and installation identifiers, screen names, app version, device and operating-system information, language/locale, session and event timestamps, ad requests and interactions, coarse location inferred from IP, crash traces, exception messages, performance and memory information, and related diagnostic identifiers. On signed-in native sessions, PlanIt sets the Firebase user ID as the Crashlytics user identifier. PlanIt attempts to redact emails, secrets, and long token-like strings from errors it explicitly records, but no automated redaction is perfect.
Support and rights requests
Your email address, correspondence, verification information that is reasonably necessary, request history, and the actions taken in response. Never send us your password.
Legal-page and network data
When you visit these static pages, Firebase Hosting and network providers necessarily receive technical request information such as IP address, browser type, requested URL, and timestamps for delivery, security, and abuse prevention. These pages do not intentionally load third-party fonts, advertising scripts, or PlanIt analytics scripts.
No active GPS collection or AI analysis.

PlanIt does not request precise-location permission and does not currently use artificial-intelligence services to analyze user content or make decisions about users. IP-derived coarse location, text you enter, links, and metadata inside files can still reveal location or other sensitive information. Do not upload sensitive data unless it is necessary and you are authorized to do so.

3. Where data comes from

  • From you: account details, schedules, tasks, contacts, notes, files, preferences, sharing choices, support messages, and permissions.
  • From your device or browser: device characteristics, local identifiers, permission status, app activity, crash information, public IP address, and local storage.
  • From sign-in providers: Google or Apple account information and authentication tokens you authorize them to provide.
  • From service providers: authentication status, analytics, ad delivery and reporting, crash reports, hosting logs, update status, and push-delivery results.
  • From another user: data about you that another user lawfully enters into a schedule or includes in a shared schedule. For example, a user may enter a teacher's name or contact details.

5. Guest Mode and local storage

In Guest Mode, PlanIt stores schedule content in device or browser storage and does not upload that guest schedule to PlanIt's Firebase account collections. Local data may include compressed or encoded values, but encoding and compression are not encryption.

Local storage may include guest or account schedule caches, Firebase authentication persistence, preferences, scheduled-notification records, widget snapshots, and cached attachments in app files, AsyncStorage, browser local storage, or IndexedDB. Other apps, browser profiles, operating-system backups, or anyone with access to an unlocked or compromised device may be able to access local data.

Guest Mode does not disable native advertising, native Firebase Analytics, Firebase Crashlytics, EAS Updates, hosting logs, or basic network processing. On the web, Firebase Analytics is not initialized unless the browser user has accepted analytics through PlanIt's cookie controls. Some services generate installation or device identifiers even without a PlanIt account.

6. Shared schedules and disclosure to other users

When you create a shared schedule, PlanIt places a sanitized copy in a separate Firestore collection and creates a code that can be used without signing into your account. Anyone who obtains or guesses a valid code may access that shared copy while it is active. Treat share codes and share links as confidential, and do not share sensitive or special-category data.

You choose an access period of 1, 3, 7, or 30 days and can remove a share from the “My Shares” area. Expiry blocks normal access but may not immediately delete the stored record. A recipient may import, copy, screenshot, forward, or independently retain information before the share is revoked or expires; PlanIt cannot remotely delete those recipient-controlled copies.

You are responsible for having a lawful basis and any required permission before entering or sharing another person's name, contact details, or other personal data.

8. Advertising, analytics, and diagnostics

Non-personalized advertising

The current native banner implementation requests non-personalized ads. Such ads are contextual rather than based on past behavior, but Google states that cookies or mobile advertising identifiers may still be used for frequency capping and aggregated reporting, and IP-derived coarse location may affect ad delivery. Device and regional privacy signals may further limit processing.

A non-personalized-ad request is not itself a consent mechanism. The current release does not provide a dedicated in-app advertising-consent interface. In regions where prior consent is required for cookies, mobile advertising identifiers, or equivalent device storage, those technologies require an appropriate consent or limited-data treatment in addition to requesting non-personalized ads.

Firebase Analytics

PlanIt records screen views and Firebase may automatically collect app-instance, device, session, and engagement events. Analytics helps us understand whether features operate and which screens are used. In the web app, optional Firebase Analytics is not initialized before affirmative analytics consent. Rejecting it does not block core features; the user can later grant or withdraw consent using the persistent Cookie settings control. Withdrawal disables future PlanIt web Analytics collection and attempts to remove accessible first-party Analytics cookies. See the Cookie & Similar Technologies Policy.

Native builds currently do not offer a dedicated Analytics switch. Uninstalling or discontinuing use stops future app-originated collection from that installation but does not automatically erase previously collected provider data. Contact us to exercise applicable objection, deletion, or withdrawal rights.

Firebase Crashlytics

Native builds use Crashlytics to collect crashes and selected non-fatal errors. Reports may contain the Firebase user ID for signed-in users, installation IDs, device and app information, stack traces, and sanitized error text. Web builds use a local console-only placeholder and do not send reports through the PlanIt Crashlytics wrapper.

9. How long we keep data

We keep personal data only for as long as reasonably necessary for the stated purpose, subject to the following criteria:

  • Account and synchronized content: generally while the account exists, until you delete particular content, or until a verified deletion request is completed.
  • Deletion-prevention marker: after an in-app deletion request, we retain the deleted Firebase user ID and server request timestamp, without email, profile content, schedules, or files. This narrow tombstone is retained as needed to prevent old authenticated sessions from recreating deleted account data and to preserve the irreversible security state.
  • Attachments: generally until you delete the file or a comprehensive account-deletion request removes the related Cloud Storage object. Cached local copies remain until cleared on each device/browser.
  • Shared schedules: normal access ends after the selected 1, 3, 7, or 30-day period or earlier deletion. Expiry alone disables normal access; it does not guarantee automatic deletion of the Firestore record. The record may remain until the owner deletes it, a comprehensive deletion request is completed, or an operational cleanup removes it.
  • Device, login, and notification records: while needed for active sessions, security, push delivery, and account history, or until removed by the user/account-deletion process.
  • Crashlytics: Firebase states that crash traces and associated identifiers are retained for 90 days before removal from live and backup systems begins.
  • Analytics: user- and event-level data follows the retention setting in our Google Analytics property and Google's retention controls. Standard Google Analytics properties offer 2- or 14-month user/event retention settings; aggregated reports may remain longer. The selected console setting is not encoded in the PlanIt client repository; contact us if you need confirmation of the setting current at the time of your request.
  • Hosting and provider logs: providers retain security and request logs under their own documented operational cycles. Firebase states that Hosting IP data is retained for a few months.
  • Support and legal records: for as long as needed to verify and document the request, resolve disputes, prevent abuse, enforce agreements, or meet legal obligations, and then deleted or de-identified.

Deletion from active systems may not immediately remove encrypted backups, immutable security logs, or provider-controlled records. Such residual data is isolated from ordinary use and removed or overwritten under the applicable backup or retention cycle unless law requires longer retention.

10. Account and data deletion

See the Account Deletion page for instructions, verification, expected timing, and a precise description of what deletion can and cannot remove.

Current in-app deletion behavior.

After recent password, Google, or Apple reauthentication, PlanIt records an irreversible deletion-prevention marker, blocks new account writes, and removes known Firestore account subcollections, legacy schedule data, registered devices, stored notifications, owner-created shared-schedule records, and objects under the account's Firebase Storage attachment path. It deletes the Firebase Authentication account only after the known cloud-data steps succeed. If a cloud step fails, the app reports the failure and keeps the sign-in record so deletion can be retried. When Apple is linked, PlanIt requires Apple reauthentication even if password or Google is also linked, because token revocation requires fresh Apple credentials.

The current-device cleanup also attempts to remove the signed-in schedule cache, cached attachments, scheduled notifications, and widget schedule data. It does not remove Guest Mode content, downloaded or exported copies, another person's imported copy, or local data on a different device.

Provider boundary.

PlanIt cannot independently certify deletion inside every provider system. Web Sign in with Apple deletion asks Firebase to revoke the Apple access token returned by reauthentication. Native iOS Apple revocation requires a trusted server-side endpoint because Apple's private credential must not be embedded in the app. PlanIt does not begin its data-deletion steps unless Apple revocation is confirmed. If confirmation fails, retry or contact planit.app.support@gmail.com. Removing PlanIt in your Apple ID settings is a separate provider-side action and does not itself delete the PlanIt account. Provider backups, diagnostics, security logs, and retention-controlled records follow the provider's own lifecycle.

Account deletion cannot erase files downloaded to a device, data imported into another user's account, screenshots or forwarded copies, operating-system backups, or local data on a device we cannot control. Clear PlanIt app/browser storage and downloaded files on every device you use.

12. Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational safeguards appropriate to the Service, including HTTPS in transit, provider encryption at rest, verified-account access rules for private Firestore and Storage paths, authentication, file-type and size limits, and redaction of selected diagnostic fields.

No method is perfectly secure. PlanIt content is not end-to-end encrypted: PlanIt's cloud providers process it to provide the Service. Encoded or compressed schedule values are not encryption. Tokenized attachment URLs and share codes can grant access to anyone who obtains them. Protect your device and account, use a unique password, keep software updated, review active devices and shares, and avoid storing highly sensitive information in PlanIt.

To report a suspected security or privacy issue, email planit.app.support@gmail.com with enough detail to investigate, but do not include passwords, authentication tokens, or unnecessary personal data.

You may find your EEA authority through the European Data Protection Board's authority list. In Ukraine, personal-data protection is supervised by the Ukrainian Parliament Commissioner for Human Rights. You may also contact the authority where you live or work.

14. Children and young users

PlanIt is a general educational organization tool and is not directed to children under 13. A person under 13 must not create an account or submit personal data. If local law sets a higher age for valid consent to online-service data processing, a user below that age must have verifiable authorization from a parent or legal guardian before using account or cloud features. In the EEA, that consent age may be between 13 and 16 depending on the country.

If you are below the age of legal majority, a parent or guardian should review these terms and help you decide what third-party information is appropriate to enter. A parent or guardian who believes a child provided data contrary to this section should contact us. We will investigate and delete or restrict the data where required.
