Effective and last updated: July 24, 2026 · Version 2.2

Choose the path that matches your situation
  • Email/password, Google, or Apple account with app access: use the in-app deletion flow and verify with a linked sign-in method. If Apple is linked, Apple verification is required so its authorization can be revoked.
  • No app access or support-assisted confirmation: send a verified email request. Support can investigate an incomplete deletion and check for provider-side residuals.
  • Guest Mode only: there is no cloud account to delete. Clear PlanIt data from every device/browser where you used it.
Deletion is permanent.

After an account is deleted, you may lose schedules, tasks, settings, files, shares, device history, and account access. This generally cannot be undone. Save any information you need before continuing.

1. Delete an account in the app

  1. Open PlanIt while signed in to the account you want to delete.
  2. Open Settings.
  3. Select Account Settings.
  4. Select Delete Account.
  5. Verify with your linked password, Google account, or Apple account.
  6. Review the final warning and confirm permanent deletion.

The app uses recent Firebase reauthentication to protect against unauthorized deletion. A successful verification remains usable for five minutes. Google and Apple verification opens the applicable provider flow; PlanIt does not ask you to create a separate password for a social-sign-in account. If Apple is linked, PlanIt requires the Apple flow even when another provider is also linked, because a new Apple token or authorization code is required for revocation.

How the current in-app flow works

After verification, PlanIt creates a minimal irreversible deletion marker to block new writes from other signed-in devices. It then deletes known Firestore account subcollections, legacy schedule data, stored notifications and devices, every shared-schedule record owned by the account, and all objects below the account's Firebase Storage attachment path. The Firebase Authentication account is deleted only after those cloud steps succeed. A failed step is reported and the sign-in record is kept so cleanup can be retried instead of being reported as a false success.

On the current device, PlanIt also attempts to clear the signed-in schedule cache, attachment cache, scheduled notifications, and widget schedule snapshot. This does not erase Guest Mode content, downloaded/exported files, another device's local storage, or recipient-controlled copies.

Sign in with Apple authorization

On web, PlanIt asks Firebase to revoke the Apple access token obtained during reauthentication before deletion starts. Native iOS returns an authorization code whose secure exchange requires a trusted server-side Apple credential. PlanIt sends that code to its configured secure revocation endpoint and does not begin deleting PlanIt data unless revocation is confirmed. If revocation cannot be confirmed, retry or contact support. You may separately remove PlanIt from your Apple ID sign-in settings, but that action alone does not delete the PlanIt account. Do not send an authorization code or token by email.

2. Request deletion by email

Send the request from the email address linked to your PlanIt account whenever possible. For Sign in with Apple, this may be an Apple private-relay address. If you cannot access that address, explain the situation; we will use a reasonable alternative verification method where possible rather than automatically rejecting the request.

Email planit.app.support@gmail.com

Your request should include:

  • the email address linked to PlanIt;
  • your sign-in method (email, Google, or Apple);
  • a clear request to delete the account and associated data;
  • known share codes or attachment details if you want us to locate residual records; and
  • an explanation if you cannot write from the linked email address.

Do not send your password, authentication token, full payment-card details, or an identity document unless we specifically explain why proportionate identity evidence is legally and operationally necessary.

3. Verification and timing

Verification protects your account from malicious deletion requests. We normally verify control of the linked email address and may ask for limited account information that only the account holder is likely to know. We use verification information only to process and document the request.

We will acknowledge a valid request and aim to complete it without undue delay, generally within 30 days. Where the GDPR applies, we respond within one month unless a permitted extension is necessary because of complexity or the number of requests; if so, we will notify you within the initial month and explain the extension. Other laws may require a different period.

If we cannot verify the request or must retain limited data, we will explain the reason and available complaint options where required by law.

4. What deletion covers

Subject to successful verification and applicable legal exceptions, a comprehensive request is intended to cover:

  • the Firebase Authentication account, email, display name, and provider links;
  • cloud schedules, tasks embedded in schedule data, settings, themes, reminders, and synchronization records stored in PlanIt's Firestore account paths;
  • registered-device records, public-IP login history, push tokens, and stored account notifications;
  • cloud attachments and related Firebase Storage metadata that can be located under the account; and
  • active, expired, or stored shared-schedule records that can be located by owner ID or supplied share code.

PlanIt retains an irreversible deletion-prevention marker containing the Firebase user ID and request timestamp, but no email address, schedule, attachment, or profile content. This narrow record prevents old authenticated sessions from recreating data after deletion and documents the security state of the deleted identifier.

We may retain the minimum information needed to comply with law, resolve or defend legal claims, prevent fraud or repeated abuse, and document that a rights request was completed. Such retained data will not be used to continue the deleted account.

5. What cannot be erased remotely

  • Local data outside the current cleanup: Guest Mode schedules, downloaded files, operating-system or browser backups, provider-managed authentication storage, and data on another device/browser.
  • Recipient-controlled copies: schedules another person imported, copied, screenshotted, downloaded, or received through a share code or system share sheet.
  • Downloaded/exported files: copies in device downloads, photo libraries, document folders, backups, cloud drives, email, or other apps.
  • Provider residuals: encrypted backups, immutable security logs, fraud-prevention records, and provider-controlled analytics or diagnostics until their retention cycles expire.
  • De-identified or aggregated statistics: information that can no longer reasonably be linked to you may be retained.

Firebase states that Crashlytics crash traces and associated identifiers are retained for 90 days before removal begins. Google Analytics user/event-level retention follows the configured property setting, while aggregated reports may remain longer. Account deletion does not automatically identify every Analytics installation record unless the necessary app-instance identifier is available.

6. Clear local or Guest Mode data

Android or iOS

  1. Delete any files you downloaded or exported from PlanIt.
  2. Use PlanIt's reset/cleanup controls where available.
  3. In the device's application settings, clear PlanIt storage/data if the platform offers that option.
  4. Uninstall PlanIt from every device. Review device or cloud backups if you need those copies removed too.

Web

  1. Sign out and close all PlanIt tabs.
  2. Use the browser's site-data controls to clear storage for the PlanIt web origin, including local storage, IndexedDB, cache, and cookies/storage created by providers.
  3. Review the Cookie & Similar Technologies Policy and use PlanIt's Cookie settings control if you only want to change optional web Analytics consent.
  4. Delete downloaded or exported files separately.

Guest Mode

Guest Mode does not create a PlanIt cloud account, so support cannot remotely identify or erase the guest schedule stored on your device. Clearing app/browser data and uninstalling are the deletion method for that content. Technical data already held by advertising, analytics, crash, hosting, update, or network providers follows the rules described in the Privacy Policy.

7. After deletion

The deleted account cannot be restored. You may later create a new account with the same email if Firebase and the sign-in provider permit it, but the new account will have a different user identity and deleted data will not automatically return. A recipient's imported schedule remains in that recipient's account unless they delete it.
